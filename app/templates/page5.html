Conclusion

Materialize

  • gradeA Lack of Expectation
    I didn't know much about Materialize before I started working with it, however, their website made things super easy to pick up. It had plenty of demonstrations and examples to work off of. Their documentation was very clean and understandable. I had no expectations going in, but I was impressed with the framework, nonetheless.
  • child_careNo Difficulties
    No difficulties whatsoever. Documentation had step-by-step instructions on how to install and configure your stuff. It was fun to scroll through all the documentation and pick and choose the cool stuff I could do. However, since the framework is very cookie-cutter, it pushes you to style and format things a certain way. This could result in similar-looking websites and less overall variation.
  • trending_downWhat Learning Curve?
    Compared to working with just plain CSS, Materialize was a real lifesaver. Everything was nicely prepackaged, making it possible to take a lot of shortcuts. I'm no professional designer, so some of my pages will still end up looking ugly, but that is by no means the framework's fault. Documentation supplied lots of examples in an easy, digestable way. It was nice.
  • access_timeIn Retrospect
    If I could do things differently, I would definitely choose to start earlier or spend more time on my pages. I'm sure you can tell that the design of my pages differ in quality because when I started out, I had no idea what I was doing, then slowly got used to the process as I practiced. I'll leave it to you to guess which page was my first lol.
  • httpNo Regrets
    I can't say I really have any regrets. Using this framework was pretty fun, and I liked all of the cool stuff I could do. It was easy to understand and I liked the aesthetic.

Flask

  • sentiment_satisfiedThings Went Ok
    Again, I had little to no expectations going in. It was fairly simple to get a grasp of structure and setup after looking at some other examples.
  • codeSome Difficulties
    I had a bit of trouble configuring everything and hosting appropriately. This was probably the hardest part of the project -- the hosting. It took me some time to figure out what went where and what needed what.
  • record_voice_overI Was Stupid
    The learning curve was a bit steeper for me compared to PHP and other frameworks, mostly due to setup and hosting. I spent more time on figuring out how to set up and host than I would've preferred. Python itself is an easy language to pick up, but getting everything to work in conjunction was a bit of a challenge.
  • refreshHindsight 20/20
    Maybe I would've picked another framework to use as a backend. Ruby on Rails, Node.js, and Django seem like much more common frameworks and I'd like to get some experience using those.
  • wb_incandescentSomeday
    Probably should've started earlier and chosen a framework more commonplace. I do appreciate the fact that Flask was overall simple to use and understand when it came to things like forms and routing.
