Frontend framework:
Materialize

Popularitymore_vert

Click image to open

A CSS framework made by Googleclose

Materalize is mostly used in the United States. According to the State of CSS report 2019, 72% of those surveyed had heard of Materialize, and 37% were interested in using it.

It was overall ranked 3rd in terms of popularity -- being beaten out by Bootstrap and Foundation. However, only 17.1% of developers say they would use it again, compared to 45% for Bootstrap.

Strengthsmore_vert
Easy and efficientclose

The built-in custom components make for easy and fast development. There are many free plugins for drop-downs, collapses and more. The framework is also very responsive, and makes for a great user experience.

Overall, Materalize is easy to pick up, with great built-in features, and ready-to-use page elements.

Weaknessesmore_vert
New, small, limitedclose

Materialize does not work on older browsers, such as anything older than Internet Explorer 10. In addition, the framework has a smaller community with less detailed documentation and forums.

Designs are also fairly limited; websites that you create may all act similarly in terms of UI.

Where it's usedmore_vert
A small communityclose

Companies such as DroneDeploy, IWB, and Greencode use Materialize. Materialize is used for a variety of different sites; mostly for advertising a company's digital services.

There don't seem to be too many large companies that use Materialize.

According to the Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2020, Ruby was named the 14th most popular programming language in the world. There are over 1M live websites that are currently using Ruby on Rails.

It is one of the more popular choices for startups, however, Ruby on Rail's popularity may be falling somewhat, in the face of other frameworks such as node.js.

Strengthsmore_vert
All around reliableclose

The language is clear, simple, and easy to learn, making for speedy development. People were finding they spent less time writing less code, while still retaining functionality. The community is also large, with vast amounts of resources to fall back on.

It also has high data protection with extra steps taken to prevent SQL injection and other attacks.

Weaknessesmore_vert
Slow and steadyclose

Ruby on Rails has much slower average runtimes compared to other languages. This could be detrimental, as according to a Google study, each second delay will decrease the number of page views.

Developers may also find an issue with the language's lack of flexibility, as you may need to go out of your way for unique customization.

Where it's usedmore_vert
Mr. Worldwideclose

Ruby on Rails is vastly popular in startups, due to its ease of use. Company giants that utilize it include GitHub, Shopify, Netflix, and Twitter. Uses include everything from e-commerce, to open-source libraries, to social media platforms.

Many big-name companies use Ruby on Rails, with possibly more to crop up.
